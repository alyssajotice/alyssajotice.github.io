Sofware Engineering Intern
JP Morgan Chase & Co

During the summer of 2018, I interned at JP Morgan Chase and Co. in their Software Engineering Intern program. Over ten weeks, I got firsthand experience seeing how technology is developed, tested, and deployed on a massive scale. I learned about the differences between developing software in a corporate environment versus a personal environment.

One of the greatest takeaways from the summer was the knowledge of how Agile works. I was placed on a sprint team with three other interns, and we operated as a fully functioning scrum team. We planned our own sprints, did backlog refinement, sprint retrospectives, and daily standups.  I learned a lot about the differences between Agile and Waterfall and the advantages and disadvantages of each.

For my project, my team worked with an internal JPMC team to create a website to centralize information needed from the team. The work I did made it easier for developers and other business analysts to find the information that they need about the health of different applications and testing environments.

Synthesizer Control Board

One of the classes I took during my freshman year was a class about musical synthesizer programming, using the programming language SuperCollider. The class gave me a lot of creative freedom to make whatever I desired for the final project. I used a Korg NanoKontroll 2 and a MIDI keyboard to build and program a sound board with changeable synthesizers.

The NanoKontroll 2 board had nine control sections that input MIDI data to the computer, so I programmed each one to play a different synth and control specific characteristics of that synth. This project was really enjoyable for me becaue I got to combine two things that interest me, music and programming! You can read more about how I executed this project in the blog post, which is here!

Nearby

During the spring of my junior year, I took CMPSC 431W at Penn State, which is the Database Management Systems class. It counts as the “writing across the curriculum” capstone class for computer science majors. The semester was spent learning about DBMSs in class and implementing a full stack web application with a database. Since this class was writing intensive, three reports were completed over the course of the semester to outline the research, creation, and implementation of the application.

My team and I built a crowd-sourced application that allowed users to post about events happening around campus. The events populated on a map based on the users location. The feed of events was algorithmically sorted based on location, voting, and tags.

Overall, I learned so much about databases during the project and became really interested in doing my thesis about a topic related to database management systems. I liked learning about concurrency control, serializability, and relational algebra. I am especially interested in query optimizers.You can read more about how I executed this project in the blog post, which is here!
